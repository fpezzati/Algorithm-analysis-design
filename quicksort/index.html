Quicksort

Divide-et-impera algorithm. Let $C$ occurrences of confrontations, $S$ occurrences of moves and $P$ occurrences of partitioning during algorithm execution. Then: $$\alpha C + \beta S + \gamma P$$ indicates total execution time, where $\alpha$, $\beta$ and $\gamma$ indicate respectively confrontation, move and partition operation cost. Quicksort takes about $2n \ln n$ confrontations and about $\frac{1}{3} n \ln n$ moves (in the worst case?). Average confrontations is expressed as: $$C_n = n + 1 + \frac{1}{n} \sum_{j=1}^{n}(C_{j-1} + C_{n-j}), C_0 = 0$$ average moves is expressed as: $$S_n = \frac{n-2}{6} + \frac{1}{n} \sum_{j=1}^{n}(S_{j-1} + S_{n-j}), S_0 = 0$$
